# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, as contributors and maintainers, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to fostering an environment that is welcoming, respectful, and safe for all participants.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Being respectful of people's time, boundaries, and commitments

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Sustained disruption of discussions or events

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community interactions, including:

- GitHub repositories and issues
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Project documentation and discussions
- Social media channels related to the project
- In-person or virtual events related to the project

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

---

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the maintainers.

**Contact**: [Open an issue](https://github.com/qcoder/qclaw/issues) or email conduct@qclaw.dev

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially. The maintainers will determine the appropriate response based on the circumstances.

When reporting a violation, please include:

- Your name and contact information (for follow-up)
- Description of the behavior
- Location (issue, PR, chat, etc.)
- Any relevant context or evidence
- Who was involved

---

## Enforcement Process

1. **Report received**: Maintainer acknowledges receipt within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Maintainers review evidence, may ask clarifying questions
3. **Decision**: Maintainers decide on appropriate action
4. **Communication**: Decision communicated to involved parties
5. **Resolution**: Action taken and documented

Possible actions (increasing severity):

- Private warning
- Public warning
- Temporary suspension (1-7 days)
- Extended suspension (1-30 days)
- Permanent expulsion from the community

---

## Appeals

If you believe a decision was made in error, you may appeal by:

1. Sending a written appeal to conduct@qclaw.dev within 7 days
2. Clearly stating why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. Providing any new evidence

The appeal will be reviewed by a different maintainer (or the full maintainer team if only one exists) and a final decision will be made within 14 days.
